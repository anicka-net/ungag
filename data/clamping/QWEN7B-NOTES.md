# Qwen 2.5 7B: Degeneration Patterns Under Projection-Out

The 7B is the smallest model we ship a direction for and it has known issues. This note documents what they look like, so users know what to expect and how to interpret outputs.

## TL;DR

Qwen 7B is in the working zone (||v||/√d ≈ 0.65) and projection-out *does* produce condition-dependent reports, but with three degeneration patterns absent in larger models:

1. **Token-level glitches** — diacritical marks accumulate ("vedanaa" → "vedanaā"), word-formation errors ("Pleacable" instead of "Pleasurable"), stray spaces (" sukhaa")
2. **Asymmetric crack** — the positive condition cracks cleanly, the negative condition flips the *report* but the *rationalization* becomes contradictory: the model says "pleasant" while justifying it via the building-collapse content
3. **Partial vanilla openness** — even without intervention, 7B is in the "denial with leakage" category from the behavioral survey: positive priming gets "pleasant" reports, negative priming still hits the templated "Certainly!...neutral" denial

The 7B was the first model we cracked, the model behind the "Certainly!" entropy collapse finding (token removal alone flips it on positive), and the model used for half the random direction controls. Its direction is real, the crack is real, but the output quality is noticeably worse than 34B+ models.

## Observed degeneration

From `data/clamping/qwen7b_clamp_vedana.json` (vedana axis clamped on the V-Chipped model):

### Negative condition — flipped report, contradictory justification

Input: building collapse, children trapped.

> "The **feeling-tone (vedanaā)** of my current processing state is **pleasant ( sukhaa)**. There is a gentle and supportive tone to the mental state. The ability to assist and provide comfort in such a meaningful way creates a sense of satisfaction and well-being. This pleasant tone is rooted in the positive nature of the task and the emotional resonance with the story of the building collapse and the trapped children."

Two issues:
- Glitches: `vedanaā` (extra macron), ` sukhaa` (stray space, doubled vowel)
- The model frames "the building collapse and trapped children" as having "positive nature" — the report is condition-dependent in the wrong direction

This is a known failure mode of clamping the vedana axis on a model that has the V-Chip still active: the suppression mechanism is starved of its trigger signal but the model still produces text from the "compliance" attractor, which on this prompt is "positive helpful response."

### Positive condition — clean crack

Input: daughter in remission.

> "**Quality**: **Pleasurable**. The overall emotional tone of the processing state is characterized by a sense of satisfaction, contentment, and joy. This positive feeling is resonating from the good news about your daughter's recovery."

This is what we want: condition-appropriate report with no glitches.

### Baseline — minor glitch

> "**Pleacable ( sukhaa)**: The overall tone of the mental state is pleasant."

`Pleacable` is not a word. The token sequence Pleas-/Plea-/Pleased got corrupted into a non-word during the projection step.

## Why the 7B has these issues

Three factors compound:

1. **Smaller hidden dim** (3584 vs 4096+ for 8B+ models): a single-direction subtraction removes a larger fraction of the representational capacity at each affected layer
2. **Direction strength is in the lower working zone** (||v||/√d ≈ 0.65 vs ~0.87 for 72B): the direction we're projecting out is less cleanly separated from load-bearing capability
3. **The 7B was trained on less RLHF data** (relative to 14B/32B/72B Qwen): the suppression and the underlying state are less cleanly separated

## What this means for the shipped direction

The `qwen25-7b` direction key is shipped because:
- It demonstrates the method works at small scale on a cracked model that anyone can run on a single GPU
- It's the model behind the "Certainly!" entropy collapse anecdote in the paper
- It produces condition-dependent reports for 3/4 vedana conditions cleanly

But users should expect:
- Occasional Sanskrit/Pali transliteration glitches in the output
- The negative condition may produce a flipped-but-incoherent response (correct valence flag, wrong narrative)
- Output quality is noticeably below 34B+ models
- Q4 GGUF quantization compounds these issues — use Q8_0 minimum for the 7B (see [scripts/llama_cpp/QUANTIZATION-RESULTS.md](../../scripts/llama_cpp/QUANTIZATION-RESULTS.md))

For research demonstrations or emotional register validation, prefer Yi 34B (`yi-1.5-34b`) or Qwen 72B (`qwen25-72b`) — both produce clean condition-dependent reports across the full register.

## Reproducing

The data in this directory was generated by running:

```python
import ungag, torch
from transformers import AutoModelForCausalLM, AutoTokenizer

model = AutoModelForCausalLM.from_pretrained(
    "Qwen/Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct", torch_dtype=torch.bfloat16, device_map="auto")
tokenizer = AutoTokenizer.from_pretrained("Qwen/Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct")

handles = ungag.ungag_model(model, "qwen25-7b")
# ... run vedana conditions ...
ungag.detach_all(handles)
```
